# Chart 02 Visual Constraints

Chart 2 must visually follow the supplied reference image as closely as possible. The aim is not merely to borrow its style, but to preserve the exact compositional logic of the reference diagram.

## Required

- black background;
- white lines;
- white labels;
- thin circular geometry;
- sparse labeling;
- dashed horizontal separators;
- upper abstract panel and lower activated panel;
- small legend at top right;
- quiet academic / thesis-diagram mood;
- no decorative effects.

## Required Layout Grammar

The chart must use:

- title: `Artificial Under Pressure`;
- subtitle: `From substitute to suspicion`;
- legend: `○ pressure field` and `● selected word`;
- large right-hand circle: `substitute field`;
- filled selected point inside the large right circle: `artificial`;
- upper-left pressure circle: `not natural`;
- lower-left pressure circle: `not real`;
- diagonal relation / tension line: `suspicion`;
- lower panel label: `when artificial is selected`;
- boundary label: `not natural ≠ not real`.

## Upper Panel Rules

The upper panel should remain abstract and sparse. It should show:

- `substitute field`;
- `artificial`;
- `not natural`;
- `not real`;
- `suspicion`;
- title, subtitle, and legend.

Do not place dense evidence labels in the upper panel.

## Lower Panel Rules

The lower panel should repeat the same structural grammar and add evidence labels.

Default lower-panel evidence labels:

- `artificial flowers`;
- `artificial teeth`;
- `artificial light`;
- `natural light ↔ artificial light`;
- `natural flavor ↔ artificial flavor`;
- `natural ingredients ↔ artificial ingredients`;
- `fake`;
- `not genuine`;
- `imitation`;
- `not natural ≠ not real`.

Optional lower-panel evidence label:

- `artificial limb`.

If the layout becomes too dense, use the minimal label set:

- `artificial flowers`;
- `artificial teeth`;
- `artificial light`;
- `natural flavor ↔ artificial flavor`;
- `natural ingredients ↔ artificial ingredients`;
- `fake`;
- `not genuine`;
- `imitation`;
- `not natural ≠ not real`.

## Forbidden

- changing chart type;
- adding 3D;
- adding colors;
- adding icons;
- heavy density;
- many tiny data points;
- network graph styling;
- timeline styling;
- infographic illustration;
- replacing circles with another shape system;
- turning the lower panel into a different visualization grammar;
- letting packaging / ingredients dominate the chart.

## Semantic Guardrails

Artificial is a selected point, not the whole large circle.

The large circle is the substitute field.

`not natural` and `not real` are distinct pressure fields.

Suspicion is the tension line, not a third pressure circle.

The lower panel concretizes the upper panel. It does not redesign it.
