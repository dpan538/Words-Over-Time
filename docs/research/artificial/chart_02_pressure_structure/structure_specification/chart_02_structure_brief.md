# Chart 02 Structure Brief

## 1. Purpose of Chart 2

Chart 2 shows how `artificial` becomes semantically pressured after the art / skill / making field established in Chart 1. It is not a chart about artificial simply becoming bad. It is a chart about `artificial` sitting inside a substitute field while being pulled toward `not natural` and `not real`, producing suspicion.

Working thesis:

```text
Artificial begins within a substitute field, but as it is pulled toward not-natural and not-real, it starts to feel suspicious.
```

## 2. Relation to Chart 1

Chart 1 showed:

```text
artificial does not simply mean fake;
it belongs to art / skill / making / contrivance.
```

Chart 2 follows that correction. It should preserve the distinction that `not natural` is not the same as `fake` or `not real`, while showing how those pressure fields begin to reshape the reading of `artificial`.

Chart 2 sits between Chart 1 and later charts. It should not become the full consumer-additives, packaging, body-replacement, AI, or synthetic-media chart.

## 3. Exact Reference-Layout Rule

The supplied reference image is the exact visual and compositional model. Chart 2 should preserve its layout grammar:

- black background;
- white linework;
- white labels;
- two stacked panels;
- upper abstract panel;
- lower activated / selected panel;
- circular semantic fields;
- selected filled point inside a large right-hand circle;
- smaller pressure circles;
- diagonal relation / tension line;
- small legend at top right;
- horizontal dashed separators;
- sparse academic / thesis-diagram mood.

This is not a loose style reference. The semantic content changes, but the composition logic should remain as close as possible.

## 5. Core Semantic Thesis

The core semantic model is:

```text
substitute field
-> artificial as selected word
-> pressure from not natural
-> pressure from not real
-> suspicion as the tension produced by those pressures
```

Suspicion is not a third pressure circle. It is a relation, tension, or vector created when `artificial` is pulled between the `not natural` and `not real` fields.

The chart must not imply:

```text
not natural = not real
```

It should explicitly preserve:

```text
not natural ≠ not real
```

## 6. Two-Panel Structure

The upper panel is the abstract semantic structure. It corresponds to the reference image's upper "possibilities" view. It contains the field geometry and minimal labels only.

The lower panel is the selected / activated state. It corresponds to the reference image's lower chosen-state view. It keeps the same spatial grammar, but reveals sparse evidence labels inside the semantic zones.

The lower panel concretizes the upper panel. It must not redesign it.
