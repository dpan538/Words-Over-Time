# Round 04 Authority Pass Findings

## Overview

This was a deliberately small broader-web pass with stronger filtering.
The search prioritized government, university, PubMed/PMC, systematic review, reference, and open textbook sources.

The pass improved support in three places:

- `artificial` versus `simulated`
- non-product evidence for the `realistic` bridge
- high-quality exit markers toward Chart 5

## Artificial vs simulated

The FCC ruling is the cleanest authority source.
It ties AI voice cloning to `artificial voice` because the technology artificially simulates a human voice.
That gives Chart 4B a strong institutional example where `artificial` and `simulated` are related but not identical.

PubMed examples broaden this beyond voice.
`Artificially simulated microgravity` and `artificially simulated maxillectomy defects` show that simulation is a controlled modeling operation.
That makes `simulated` a narrower representational basin, not a synonym for fake.

## Realistic bridge

The strongest new bridge support is biomedical.
PubMed and NIH sources describe realistic or mechanically faithful artificial skin models used for training, disease modeling, and drug testing.
This is stronger than product-page evidence because the artificial object is valued precisely for resembling or modeling real human tissue.

The key point is unchanged but better supported:

- artificial can be realistic
- realistic does not mean real
- realistic does not mean fake

## Human-process exit markers

Britannica's `artificial life` entry supports life as a process-oriented exit.
The Springer systematic review on artificial companions supports companion as a stable research term with adaptivity, engagement, emotional bonds, and long-term human-machine relationships.

These are useful for Chart 4B only as exits.
They should prepare Chart 5 without taking over Chart 4B.

## What did not improve

`Realistic artificial intelligence` remains weak.
The broader web did not produce a stable high-quality source for that exact phrase.
It should stay out of the core Chart 4B evidence model.
