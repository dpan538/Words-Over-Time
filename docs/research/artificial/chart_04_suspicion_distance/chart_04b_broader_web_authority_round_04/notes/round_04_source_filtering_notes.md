# Round 04 Source Filtering Notes

## Filter used

Preferred sources:

- government and regulatory sources
- university research news
- PubMed and PMC records
- systematic reviews
- edited reference sources
- open textbooks

Deprioritized sources:

- SEO comparison pages
- generic product pages
- thin blog posts
- hype-oriented AI commentary
- reposts of government materials where the government source was available

## Why this mattered

The broader web can add support, but only if source quality improves.
For `realistic artificial skin`, broad search did improve quality because it found biomedical and NIH evidence.
For `artificial vs simulated`, broad search improved quality because it found FCC and PubMed examples.
For `realistic artificial intelligence`, broad search did not improve quality because results remained ambiguous or hype-skewed.

## Recommended evidence hierarchy now

Use first:

- UBC open textbook for artificial versus fake as separate axes
- FCC for artificial voice and simulated voice
- PubMed/NIH/PMC for realistic artificial skin and modeled tissue
- Britannica for artificial life exit
- Springer systematic review for artificial companion exit

Use only as supporting texture:

- product pages
- lifestyle snippets
- commercial AI pages
