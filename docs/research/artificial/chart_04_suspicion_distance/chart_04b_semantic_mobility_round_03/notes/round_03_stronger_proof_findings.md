# Round 03 Stronger Proof Findings

## 1. Stronger proof for artificial vs fake

The strongest new source is the UBC AI text.
It does not merely define words separately.
It states that `real/fake` and `natural/artificial` are different distinctions.

That matters because it gives Chart 4B a more rigorous conceptual foundation:

- something can be artificial and still real
- something can be fake and either artificial or natural

This is substantially stronger than a thesaurus list or a one-off example.

The exact phrase sources still matter.
`Artificial but not fake` remains valuable because it states the distinction plainly in live prose.
But the UBC source gives the best theoretical backing for why that phrase is not just rhetorical.

## 2. Stronger proof for realistic as a bridge

The University of Oregon artificial skin material is the best upgrade in this round.
It is not retail copy.
It is research language about an artificial construct that more accurately mimics real human skin and even looks like real skin.

This directly supports the key bridge logic:

- artificial
- realistic
- not identical with real

The PLOS One voice clone study strengthens this on the process side.
Some AI-generated voices and voice clones are judged similarly real or human as real recordings, even without a full hyperrealism effect.
So realism is not limited to static objects like flowers or eyes.
It extends into voice and perception.

## 3. Stronger proof for simulated

The FCC ruling is the strongest new source here.
It states that voice cloning falls under `artificial voice` because the technology artificially simulates a human voice.

This helps refine the basin logic:

- `artificial` is the broader made or generated category
- `simulated` names a narrower representational or mimetic operation inside that space

That is a much better basis for Chart 4B than leaving `simulated` at dictionary level only.

## 4. What this changes

Round 03 makes it easier to justify:

- fake as a drift target rather than synonym
- realistic as a bridge attractor
- simulated as a narrower representational neighbor

It does not remove every uncertainty, but it strengthens the center of the model enough to proceed.
