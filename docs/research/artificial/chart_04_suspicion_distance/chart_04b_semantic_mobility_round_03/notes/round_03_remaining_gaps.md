# Round 03 Remaining Gaps

## Remaining weak spots

- direct everyday corpus pairings of `artificial` and `simulated` are still lighter than the institutional and technical sources
- `realistic artificial intelligence` is still too sparse and should not be forced into the visual model
- some embodiment examples such as teeth and eyes remain less authoritative than skin and voice

## Risks that remain

- the strongest conceptual evidence now comes partly from AI and cognition discussions, so later prose should avoid sounding as if Chart 4B is only about AI
- medical and research examples may overweight embodiment and biotechnical domains
- the bridge evidence is much better, but still not equally distributed across all object types

## Bottom line

The important gaps are now narrower than before.
What remains is mostly edge-cleaning rather than central-claim rescue.
