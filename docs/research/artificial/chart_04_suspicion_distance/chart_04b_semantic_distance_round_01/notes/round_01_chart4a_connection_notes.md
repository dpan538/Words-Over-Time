## 1. How 4A negative charge relates to fake adjacentness

Chart 4A showed that `artificial` can accumulate negative charge across domains such as emotion manners additives and labeling discourse.
That negative charge helps explain why `artificial` can drift toward the fake basin.
It does not prove synonymy.

## 2. Why pejoration does not equal synonymy with fake

Pejoration tracks evaluative shading.
Semantic distance tracks lexical relation.
A word can become negatively colored while still retaining a broad neutral branch.
That is exactly what the evidence suggests for `artificial`.

## 3. How semantic distance can prevent overclaiming

Chart 4B can stop 4A from overreaching.
Instead of saying that `artificial` simply became `fake`, we can say that:

- some uses pull it toward fake
- some uses keep it near synthetic or constructed
- some uses place it near realistic or simulated

That framing is more faithful to the evidence.

## 4. What 4A should not say before 4B

Chart 4A should not imply:

- that `artificial` and `fake` are interchangeable
- that all negative charge is deception language
- that `natural`, `real`, and `genuine` are the same contrast category
- that `realistic` means `real`
