# Round 01 Semantic Distance Findings

## 1. Overall semantic-distance conclusion

`Artificial` should be modeled as a semantic zone or trajectory.
Its center lies near made and non natural meanings.
From there it can drift toward synthetic, imitation, simulation, and in some contexts fake adjacentness.

## 2. Which terms are closest to artificial

The closest neighbors in this pass are:

- `constructed`
- `manufactured`
- `synthetic`
- `imitation`
- `simulated`

These are not all equally close for the same reason.
`Constructed` and `manufactured` stay near the neutral made core.
`Synthetic` stays near the material and additive branch.
`Imitation` and `simulated` sit closer to resemblance and substitution.

## 3. Which terms are contrast terms

The strongest contrast anchors are:

- `natural`
- `real`
- `genuine`
- `authentic`

These terms define the field against which artificial is measured.
They should not be collapsed into one another, but they do cluster as the opposite side of the map.

## 4. Which terms pull artificial toward fake

The strongest pull terms are:

- `fake`
- `counterfeit`
- `sham`
- `false`

That pull is strongest in affective and sincerity contexts such as `artificial tears` and `artificial manners`.
It is much weaker in neutral built or regulatory contexts.

## 5. Which terms pull artificial toward realistic or simulated

The strongest bridge terms are:

- `realistic`
- `simulated`

These terms let artificial things be lifelike or convincingly rendered without becoming real.
This is a major reason the future visual should preserve a bridge field between `artificial` and `real`.

## 6. Which terms pull artificial toward synthetic or constructed

The strongest pull terms are:

- `synthetic`
- `constructed`
- `manufactured`
- `processed`

These terms anchor artificial in material production industrial processing and non natural fabrication.

## 7. Why artificial should not collapse into fake

- It still has a large neutral made branch.
- It can name regulated industrial categories with no fraud implication.
- Its fake adjacent uses are real but partial.
- Historical dictionaries already preserve separate not natural and not genuine senses.

## 8. Whether an attractor-field visualization is justified

Yes.
This pass supports a basin based or attractor field model more than a single straight line model.
The strongest candidate basins are:

- made and constructed
- synthetic material
- imitation and substitute
- simulated and realistic
- fake and not genuine
- natural and real
- genuine and authentic
- processed consumer

The visualization should still carry visible uncertainty around `simulated`, `realistic`, and the fake edge.
