# Round 01 Context Findings

## 1. Overview

Context evidence confirms that `artificial` can move across multiple semantic basins.
The strongest contexts do not support a simple synonymy claim with `fake`.
They support a drift model.

## 2. Contexts where artificial is fake adjacent

- `artificial tears` is the cleanest early example of feigned emotional display.
- `artificial manners` clearly points to affectation rather than genuine feeling.
- In these uses `artificial` is close to `sham`, `feigned`, and `not genuine`.

## 3. Contexts where artificial is clearly not fake

- `artificial satellite` is neutral human made language with no deception.
- Regulatory phrases such as `artificial flavor` and `artificial color` are industrial categories rather than fraud labels.
- These uses keep `artificial` near made or synthetic rather than fake.

## 4. Contexts where artificial is realistic

- `realistic artificial skin` is the strongest bridge example in this pass.
- `realistic artificial flowers` shows similar modern use in consumer and decorative contexts.
- These contexts show that `realistic` measures convincing resemblance, not genuineness.

## 5. Contexts where artificial is synthetic or manufactured

- Food coloring and flavoring examples tie `artificial` to additives and industrial processing.
- FDA and CFR language keep that category visible in present day labeling discourse.
- This is where `artificial` is closest to `synthetic`, `manufactured`, and `processed`.

## 6. Contexts where artificial is imitation or substitute

- `artificial flowers` and related object uses align with the substitute branch.
- `Imitation leather` helps define the neighboring basin because it foregrounds copying a genuine article.
- These uses are often fake adjacent but not always deceptive.

## 7. Contexts that remain ambiguous

- Direct `artificial vs fake` corpus pairings remain thinner than hoped.
- `Simulated` still needs wider evidence outside dictionary examples.
- `Artificial but genuine` and several other exact phrase searches were sparse.
