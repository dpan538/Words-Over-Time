# Round 01 Dictionary Findings

## 1. Overview

Dictionary evidence strongly supports a non collapsed model.
`Artificial` is not a stable synonym of `fake`.
Instead it bundles at least three live branches:

- made by human skill and not natural
- imitation or substitute
- forced feigned or affected

That internal branching is the core reason Chart 4B should treat `artificial` as a trajectory.

## 2. Artificial vs fake

`Fake` is more tightly organized around non genuineness counterfeit status and deception.
`Artificial` can enter that territory, especially in phrases such as `artificial tears` or `artificial manners`, but it also has many uses with no fraud component at all.
The clearest dictionary contrast is that `artificial` still comfortably names neutral human made objects such as `artificial satellite`.

## 3. Artificial vs synthetic

`Synthetic` is closer than `fake`.
Its center is material production through chemical process by human agency.
That makes it a strong near neighbor for additive and industrial uses of `artificial`.
But `synthetic` does not cover the full sincerity and affectation range carried by `artificial`.

## 4. Artificial vs imitation

`Imitation` presupposes a model or original being copied.
That makes it a strong side neighbor when `artificial` names substitute objects such as flowers or leather.
But `artificial` can also simply mean human made without copying any original.

## 5. Artificial vs simulated

`Simulated` is representational and modeled.
It often means made to look genuine or staged to reproduce some process or event.
That places it between `artificial` and `realistic`, not at the neutral built core of `artificial`.

## 6. Real vs realistic

This is one of the most important distinctions.
`Realistic` means convincingly rendered to appear natural or real.
It can therefore modify artificial things.
`Real` instead marks actuality genuineness or non imitation.
So a thing can be `realistic artificial skin` without being real skin.

## 7. Genuine vs authentic

Both terms oppose fake like uses, but they do not do identical work.
`Genuine` stresses claimed origin character and sincerity.
`Authentic` adds authority trustworthiness or faithful relation to an original.
For Chart 4B this means they belong near each other but do not need to be merged.

## 8. Natural vs artificial

`Natural` is the strongest lexical opposite to `artificial` in origin terms.
It also carries spontaneity and minimally processed meanings in modern food discourse.
But `natural` should not simply be equated with `genuine`.
Origin and sincerity are related but not identical dimensions.

## 9. Main dictionary limitations

- Dictionary synonym clusters can flatten important differences.
- Dictionaries do not show how often each sense is active in each domain.
- `Fake`, `real`, `natural`, and `genuine` are all broad enough to mislead if treated as single point terms.
- `Simulated` still needs more non dictionary contextual checking.
