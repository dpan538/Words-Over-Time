# Round 01 Source Notes

## Dictionary and lexical sources

- WordReference was the most efficient modern source for compact multi sense boundaries.
- Merriam-Webster was especially useful for `fake`, `counterfeit`, `simulated`, `realistic`, and `authentic`.
- Webster 1828 gave strong checkpoint support for the split inside `artificial`.
- Etymonline remains useful as a chronology pointer but not final dating proof.

## Historical and corpus style sources

- Project Gutenberg continues to provide the strongest early usage anchors for `artificial tears` and `artificial manners`.
- Chronicling America remains the best accessible bridge into consumer and additive suspicion language.
- FDA and 21 CFR sources are strong for active label categories and absence claims.

## Source quality judgment

- Highest confidence: WordReference entries; Merriam-Webster entries; Webster 1828 checkpoints; Project Gutenberg anchors; FDA and CFR materials.
- Medium confidence: Etymonline chronology; Chronicling America OCR based snippets until image review.
- Lower confidence: modern news and product snippets used only to show that `realistic artificial` collocations are alive.

## Source balance

This pass is intentionally mixed:

- dictionary meaning
- historical dictionary checkpoint
- literary snippet
- newspaper and advertising snippet
- regulatory labeling language
- light modern collocation checking

That mix is enough for visual planning evidence, but not yet enough for maximal public facing claims.
