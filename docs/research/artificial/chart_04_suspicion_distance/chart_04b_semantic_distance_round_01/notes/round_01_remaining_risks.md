# Round 01 Remaining Risks

## Core risks

- Broad unigram ambiguity means baseline frequency cannot stand in for semantic proof.
- Dictionary definitions do not automatically reflect live usage proportions.
- Thesaurus synonym clusters overstate equivalence and flatten basin boundaries.
- Snippet evidence remains sparse for several exact phrase searches.
- Visual distance will remain partly interpretive even with strong evidence.

## Specific conceptual risks

- Fake adjacent does not mean fake.
- Realistic and real must not be collapsed.
- Natural and genuine must not be collapsed.
- Synthetic and artificial are close without being identical.
- Imitation and counterfeit should not be treated as the same strength of deception.

## Practical risks before public claims

- A few newspaper and advertising snippets still need image level verification before direct quotation.
- `Simulated` still needs wider corpus support outside dictionary examples.
- Modern `realistic artificial` evidence is enough for planning but still somewhat thin.
- Direct `artificial vs fake` corpus comparisons are thinner than the dictionary boundary evidence.
