# Round 02 Direct Contrast Findings

## 1. Artificial vs fake

This contrast is much stronger after Round 02.
The exact phrase `artificial but not fake` now appears in at least two usable modern sources.
That matters because it does not merely imply a distinction. It states one.

At the same time, earlier evidence still shows that `artificial` can move toward `fake` in narrower contexts such as `artificial tears` and `artificial manners`.
So the best conclusion remains:

- `artificial` can become fake adjacent
- `artificial` does not collapse into `fake`

## 2. Artificial vs synthetic

This contrast is also stronger.
The clearest modern pattern is:

- `synthetic` marks method or chemical production
- `artificial` marks a broader human made or labeling category

That keeps `synthetic` close to `artificial` while preserving its narrower material focus.

## 3. Artificial vs imitation

The evidence still favors a partial distinction:

- `imitation` assumes a model or genuine source being copied
- `artificial` can name substitutes, but can also simply mean made by humans

The substitute branch is real, but it does not define all uses of `artificial`.

## 4. Artificial vs simulated

This remains weaker than the other core contrasts.
The definition evidence is solid:

- `simulated` is modeled, staged, or made to look genuine
- `artificial` is broader

But the direct contextual evidence is still thinner than for `fake`, `synthetic`, or `realistic`.

## 5. Artificial vs realistic

This is now one of the strongest contrasts in the file set.
`Realistic` repeatedly modifies artificial objects or media:

- skin
- teeth
- eyes
- voice
- plants
- limbs

That means `realistic` is not a synonym of `real`.
It is the vocabulary of convincing resemblance.

## 6. Artificial vs natural / real / genuine / authentic

These remain strong contrast fields, but they do different work.

- `natural` is the origin contrast
- `real` is the actuality and genuineness contrast
- `genuine` is the sincerity and claimed-origin contrast
- `authentic` adds trust and provenance

Round 02 did not erase those differences. It made them more important.

## 7. Which contrasts are evidence-backed

Best backed:

- artificial vs fake
- artificial vs synthetic
- artificial vs realistic
- artificial vs natural
- artificial vs real

Well backed but less directly:

- artificial vs genuine
- artificial vs authentic

## 8. Which contrasts remain weak

- artificial vs simulated
- artificial vs imitation as a modern corpus contrast
- artificial vs false as a high value comparison
