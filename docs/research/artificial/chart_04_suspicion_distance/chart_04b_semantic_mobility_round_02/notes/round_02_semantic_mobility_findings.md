# Round 02 Semantic Mobility Findings

## 1. Overall findings

Round 02 strengthens the mobility thesis.
`Artificial` behaves less like a point and more like a mobile semantic operator that shifts with object type, process type, and domain.

## 2. Is artificial a point or a trajectory?

It is better treated as a trajectory.
The evidence now supports motion across at least four strong basins:

- made and constructed
- synthetic and material
- imitation and substitute
- simulated and realistic

From there it can drift toward fake adjacentness or toward human-process exits.

## 3. Which basins are strongest?

Strongest basins now are:

- made_constructed
- synthetic_material
- simulated_realistic
- fake_deceptive as a drift target

`Natural/real` and `genuine/authentic` remain strong contrast fields rather than destination basins for `artificial`.

## 4. Which basins are weak?

Weakest or least stabilized:

- imitation_substitute as a modern corpus basin
- simulated beyond dictionary and bridge cases
- human_process_exit at its outer edge, especially consciousness and companion

## 5. Does artificial collapse into fake?

No.
Round 02 strengthens the opposite conclusion.
The best new evidence literally states `artificial but not fake`.
That direct phrasing is more valuable than any loose synonym cluster.

## 6. How does artificial move toward human-process terms?

It moves by extension from things and materials toward functions and capacities:

- from artificial skin and limbs
- to artificial voice and language
- to artificial life and intelligence

That path shows semantic expansion, not just pejoration.

## 7. Is the attractor-field model justified?

Yes.
It is now better justified than a single linear distance model.
The evidence is still uneven, but it strongly favors an attractor-field structure with central mobility rather than a fixed point map.
