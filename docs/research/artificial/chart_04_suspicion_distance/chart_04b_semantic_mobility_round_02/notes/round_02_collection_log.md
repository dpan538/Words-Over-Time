# Round 02 Collection Log

## Overview

This round extended Chart 4B from semantic distance into semantic mobility.
The task was to strengthen direct contrasts, reinforce the `realistic` bridge, and add only a small set of Chart 5 exit markers.

## What was read first

- Round 01 processed files for definitions, basins, relation scores, contexts, collocations, rationale, visual candidates, and readiness.
- Round 01 notes for interpretation limits and prior gaps.
- Round 03 and Round 04 bridge materials to avoid duplicating work.

## What was newly collected

- Direct exact phrase evidence for `artificial but not fake`.
- Stronger modern distinction between `artificial` and `synthetic`.
- Additional `realistic artificial *` collocations.
- Exit leads for `artificial voice`, `artificial language`, `artificial life`, `artificial intelligence`, and neighboring human process terms.

## Main result

`Artificial` is better supported as a mobile semantic operator than as a fixed point.
Round 02 especially strengthens:

- fake as drift target rather than synonym
- synthetic as nearby material basin
- realistic as a bridge basin
- human process terms as exit markers rather than core 4B nodes

## Main caution

The strongest new evidence is still uneven.
Some of it is excellent exact phrasing.
Some of it is product or explanatory prose rather than balanced corpus prose.
